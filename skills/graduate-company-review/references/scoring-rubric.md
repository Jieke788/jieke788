# Scoring Rubric

Use this rubric to keep company assessments consistent for new graduates.

## Dimensions

Score each dimension from 1 to 10. Use whole numbers unless the evidence clearly supports a half-step.

- `薪资竞争力`: Compare the company's visible salary range with similar companies, roles, and cities for fresh graduates.
- `成长空间`: Judge training quality, role ceiling, learning speed, manager exposure, and whether the role builds transferable experience.
- `业务前景`: Judge whether the business appears to have realistic demand, a clear commercial model, and a credible position in its market.
- `稳定性`: Judge layoffs, business continuity, funding pressure, legal or compliance signals, and whether the company appears operationally stable.
- `工作强度风险`: Higher score means lower risk. Reduce the score when overtime, weekend work, or frequent emergencies appear common.
- `管理与口碑风险`: Higher score means lower risk. Reduce the score when there are repeated signals of poor management, arbitrary culture, or abusive practices.
- `校招友好度`: Judge whether the company has campus hiring structure, onboarding support, junior-friendly expectations, or a track record of training graduates.
- `岗位匹配度`: Judge how well the specific role fits the user's major, internships, career direction, and current competitiveness.

## Evidence rules

- Base the score on current, attributed evidence whenever possible.
- Prefer cross-source consistency over any single dramatic data point.
- If only weak signals exist, score conservatively and lower confidence.
- Do not fill missing data with invented assumptions.

## Suggested interpretation

- `8-10`: clearly positive on this dimension
- `6-7`: usable or mixed-positive
- `4-5`: meaningful concern or limited upside
- `1-3`: strong negative signal

## Overall verdict

Use the eight dimensions to form a practical verdict rather than a fake mathematical certainty.

Default guidance:

- `适合`: most dimensions are positive, no severe red flag, and the role is defensible for a new graduate
- `谨慎`: mixed evidence, limited upside, or meaningful uncertainty that requires comparison with alternatives
- `不推荐`: strong red flags, weak business fundamentals, poor graduate fit, or repeated risk signals

If the evidence is thin, let confidence drop faster than the verdict strength rises.

## Confidence levels

- `高`: multiple strong and recent sources agree on the core facts
- `中`: some key facts are supported, but salary, culture, or risk evidence is incomplete
- `低`: limited public information, conflicting signals, or mostly weak sources
