# Role and Salary Guidance

Use this file to normalize role coverage and salary wording across different company assessments.

## Role families

Support all common graduate role families. Map the user's wording into one of these when useful:

- `研发与技术`: backend, frontend, full-stack, mobile, test, data, algorithm, devops, security
- `产品`: product manager, product operations, strategy product, AI product
- `设计`: UI, UX, visual, motion, industrial design
- `运营`: user operations, content operations, growth, community, e-commerce operations
- `市场与品牌`: marketing, brand, PR, event, media, content marketing
- `销售与商务`: sales, BD, account, channel, customer success, pre-sales
- `职能支持`: HR, recruiting, finance, legal, procurement, admin
- `供应链与制造`: planning, quality, production, procurement, logistics
- `客服与服务`: customer service, implementation, after-sales, support

## Salary expression

Express salary by:

- role
- city
- experience level, defaulting to fresh graduate or 0-1 year

Prefer these formats:

- `上海产品助理校招样本多集中在 X-Y`
- `深圳前端校招公开样本有限，保守看在 X-Y 区间`
- `该公司北京市场岗样本不足，只能判断大致不低于同城中位`

Do not claim an exact average unless the public sample quality is genuinely solid.

## City differences

Reflect city differences when the role or company operates in multiple cities. At minimum:

- distinguish tier-1 city ranges from lower-cost cities when evidence supports it
- avoid transplanting one city's salary directly to another city
- mention relocation or local-office differences if they materially affect the decision

## Sparse data fallback

When public salary samples are insufficient:

- explicitly write `样本不足`
- do not force an average salary
- provide either a cautious range or a directional statement
- lower confidence in the final result

## Graduate-fit rules

When judging salary and role fit for graduates:

- separate `适合入行` from `薪资很高`
- do not over-reward a company just because one role pays above market if training, workload, or stability looks poor
- treat structured onboarding, clear junior expectations, and skill accumulation as meaningful positives
- reduce role-fit scores when the role obviously expects prior project depth, heavy KPI pressure, or immediate independent delivery beyond typical graduate readiness
