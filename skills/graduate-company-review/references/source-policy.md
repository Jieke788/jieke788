# Source Policy

Use this policy to keep judgments rigorous, real, and reliable.

## Source priority

Use sources in this order when available:

1. 风鸟 and similar structured company-profile sources
2. company official site, careers page, campus recruitment page, and official public statements
3. public job listings and salary samples from major recruiting platforms
4. reputable business news, encyclopedia entries, and industry research
5. employee reviews, forums, and social posts as weak risk signals only

## What counts as strong evidence

Treat these as strong evidence when current and attributable:

- official company business descriptions
- active or recent job postings
- public campus recruitment pages
- recent news about funding, layoffs, lawsuits, shutdown risk, or expansion
- repeated salary ranges from multiple recruiting sources

## What counts as weak evidence

Treat these only as supporting signals:

- single employee comments
- anonymous forum threads
- old salary screenshots
- undated reposts or aggregation pages with no primary backing

Do not convert weak evidence into definite statements.

## Conflict handling

If sources disagree:

- say `存在分歧`
- identify which point is disputed, such as salary range, overtime intensity, or current business momentum
- prefer the more current and more attributable source
- keep the final conclusion conservative

## Time sensitivity

Always assume these can change quickly and must be checked live:

- salary
- hiring status
- business condition
- layoffs
- city-specific openings

If current evidence is unavailable, explicitly say the information may be outdated.

## Employee-review usage

Employee-review style sources can be used to flag possible risks such as:

- overtime pressure
- manager instability
- high turnover
- process chaos

But they cannot by themselves prove that the company is bad or unsuitable.

## Output discipline

- Distinguish `事实`, `推断`, and `传闻/弱信号`.
- Prefer missing data over fabricated certainty.
- When evidence is sparse, lower confidence and avoid strong recommendations.
